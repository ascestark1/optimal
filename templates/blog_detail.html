Gonorrhea: Symptoms, Causes, Treatment, and Prevention Guide

Written By:

Gonorrhea is a sexually transmitted bacteria infection it’s spread by sexual contact or transmitted through birth.

#1. SIGNS AND SYMPTOMS

In women ,Vaginal discharge that resembles pus and has a bad odor.

  • Pain when urinating
  • Inter menstrual period
  • Dyspareunia(painful intercourse)
  • Lower abdominal pains
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Nausea.

    • In men:—burning sensation when urinating

  • Urethral discharge that resembles pus and sometimes with blood.
  • swelling and pain in one testicle
  • Reduced urine stream
  • Pain in the anus or rectum
  • discharge from the rectum

    • Gonorrhea is treated with antibiotics. It’s important to take all prescribed medications.
    -Avoid sex for at least 7 days after completion of medication

    #2. COMPLICATIONS

  • Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID): A condition that can occur in women when gonorrhea spreads to the reproductive organs. PID can cause infertility, ectopic pregnancy, and long-term pelvic pain.
  • Epididymitis: A painful inflammation of the testicles that can lead to infertility.
  • Urethritis: An inflammation of the urethra that can cause burning when urinating.
  • Prostatitis: An inflammation of the prostate gland that can cause pain in the groin and pelvis, or discomfort when urinating.
  • Conjunctivitis: An eye infection that can occur in babies born to mothers with gonorrhea. If left untreated, it can lead to blindness.
  • Disseminated gonococcal infection: A rare condition that occurs when gonorrhea spreads through the bloodstream and infects multiple organs, such as the heart, joints, and skin.
  • Arthritis: A rare but serious complication that can occur when gonorrhea spreads to the bloodstream.
  • Heart valve damage: A rare but serious complication that can occur when gonorrhea spreads to the bloodstream.
  • Increased risk of HIV/AIDS: People with gonorrhea are more likely to get HIV, the virus that causes AIDS.

    • #3. PREVENTION

    Use a condom during all sexual intercourse

  • Limit the number of sexual partners
  • Get tested for sexually transmitted infections before having sex with a new partner.
  • Consider regular sexually transmitted infections screening.
